About me

Hi, my name is Oli and I want to make web apps that are awesome for everyone.

For the last three years I have worked on a community project called digital inclusion, which aims to teach essential skills and provide opportunities for people who have been excluded from technology.

The project got me working with computers, mobile phones and the internet every day. There was something magical about showing people what they could do with modern tech. During the pandemic I wanted to make a website to publicly store resources for coaching work and this led me to build my first website using Wix. It was this experience that led me to the world of HTML, CSS, JavaScript and ultimately to where I am now in my coding journey.

Throughout the project i've gained an acute awareness that many web and software applications fail the very people who could benefit from such technologies the most. I want to change that and build software that is useful, fast and enjoyable for all.

Check out my Inclusive development page to see some examples of inclusive web development in practice.

The second focus of my application site is 'mental health', a topic that is very important to me. As part of my public involvement in research work at the McPin Foundation I have seen a flurry of development in the field of technology for mental health and wellbeing, including a mobile app to help with paranoia, and a virtual reality therapy for psychosis. I would love to develop in this space in the future using the skills from the apprencticeship to create quality software that has an impact.

Check out my Digital health page to explore a digital health tool I built with JavaScript.

I believe that I'm a good fit for the Founders and Coders programme because I've come to realise through training, studying and working that the times I have been most motivated, engaged and resilient have been the times when I truly felt part of a group. I enjoy explaining and exploring code with others just as much as learning, following and building. The coaching groups have given me insight into the co-operative and collaborative ethos of the programme and has helped me build the confidence to share and critically reflect on my code and my thinking.

I have spent most of my life working and studying in the healthcare and voluntary sector and I greatly enjoy helping others. I understand that web development involves delivering products and that companies have commercial goals and plans, but I also strongly believe in the social good that software and technology can promise. The projects at the 'Tech for Better' summer showcase were inspiring to me and align exactly with what type of work I would like to do in the future, I feel that Founders and Coders is a community that will help me be a 'good' developer in the very broadest sense.
