Mighty Mushrooms 🍄 🍄 🍄

My fascination with fungi! 🌳🍂🌿

Summer has a lot going for it, it's warmer, it's brighter and people tend to enjoy life more when the sun is beaming in the sky.

But there are many who much prefer the damp, cool, darkness of autumn, who would prefer to bury themselves in earth than face the scorching heat... I am of course talking about mushrooms!

Mushrooms are really interesting lifeforms, they were here long before us (the oldest fungi fossil is dated at 1 Billion years old) and they may well be here long after us because they are diverse, resilient and very adaptive to different enviroments.

When I tell people I like to go foraging for mushrooms people assume that I'm the hippie type...but I feel the enjoyment of exploring for mushrooms is that i'm not looking for anything at all, you just never know what you might find under a log or peeking out from a patch of leaves, it's the element of surprise that makes it so fun.

Most mushrooms are easier on the eye than the stomach so I tend to just take pictures of them, you can check out some of my photos on this page and also in this album: ⬇️

Now that I have your attention, here are some other great facts about our mushie companions:

  1. Taxol is one of the most widely used chemotherapies and orginates from fungi that lives within Yew trees 🏥
  2. NASA are considering using mushrooms to build houses in space 🚀
  3. Some varieties of mushrooms can glow in the dark 🌠
  4. Oyster Mushrooms are used for recycling waste materials and can survive on a diet of cigarette butts 🚬
